# Stage 2 Interview Notes — Hyderabad Cohort
**Round:** Usability testing on MVP 1  
**Location:** Hyderabad, India  
**Format:** Think-aloud walkthrough of live app + follow-up questions  
**Participants:** 10 (mix of students, early-career, and mid-career professionals)  
**Interviewer:** Daria Karmazin  

---

## Participant 1 — Arjun R.
**Age:** 24 | **Profession:** Software Engineer, TCS (HITEC City) | **Housing status:** In PG, wants to move to shared flat | **Stage:** Early career, 8 months in Hyderabad

**General Q1 — Good flatmate fit?**
"Mainly cleanliness and sleep schedule. I have early morning standups so I need things quiet by 10pm." Non-negotiable: cleanliness, sleep schedule. Flexible: food preferences, occasional guests.

**General Q2 — How do you decide?**
"I check work timings first — similar schedules matter a lot. Then I talk on WhatsApp for a bit before agreeing. Employer name, where they're from, whether anyone can vouch for them — those are the things I look for."

**General Q3 — Concerns?**
"Wasting time is the biggest one. On NoBroker I talked to 10 people and most ghosted me or the flat was already gone. I'd also want to know if someone has a history of not paying rent."

**General Q4 — When would you use this?**
"When my PG contract ends or when I relocate for a new project. I usually start looking 3–4 weeks before I need to move."

---

**R2 Q1 — Onboarding think-aloud:**
Name, email, password — no issues. [Paused at housing_status dropdown] "Wait, what does 'EITHER' mean? Either what? I'd pick LOOKING but I'm not sure if that means I'm looking for a flat or a flatmate." After submitting: "I expected profiles to appear right away. Nothing told me I was done with setup."

**R2 Q2 — Profile creation:**
"The fields are ok but missing my company — that's the first thing people ask in Hyderabad. Also no veg/non-veg field, which is a big thing here. And the budget field shows a £ sign — that's wrong, should be ₹ or at least $."

**R2 Q3 — Discovery page:**
"So these are other people looking? The card doesn't show their job or area. Name and age is not enough — I need to know which part of Hyderabad they're targeting. Gachibowli and Ameerpet are completely different for me."

**R2 Q4 — Like/Pass:**
"I clicked Like and... nothing happened? Oh wait something flashed. I expected it to slide to the next card automatically, like Tinder." Expected: animated transition. Got: static update.

**R2 Q5 — Match overlay:**
"Oh this appeared! Ok so we matched — I can chat now?" [Understood concept] "I thought it would automatically open a chat. Had to find Messages myself."

**R2 Q6 — Messages:**
"The name shows but I can't remember this person's profile. Can I click the name to see their profile again? No? That's annoying."

**R2 Q7 — Sending a message:**
"I typed and hit enter... did it send? [3-second wait] The input bar didn't clear right away so I thought it failed." Small delay created uncertainty.

**R2 Q8 — Overall:**
Strongest: the matching concept is clean and simple. Frustrating: no way to filter by area, £ symbol is wrong, can't navigate back to a profile from chat.

**R2 Q9 — Would you use again?**
"Maybe if it had filters and more users. NoBroker has more people right now so it's more useful, but this is cleaner."

**R2 Q10 — One change:**
"Add a neighbourhood filter. I don't care about someone in Secunderabad if I'm working in Gachibowli. That's the first thing everyone checks."
